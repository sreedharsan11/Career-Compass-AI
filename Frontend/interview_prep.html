Interview Preparation

Use this page as a quick revision guide before interviews – HR, technical, and general tips.

Common HR Questions

  • Tell me about yourself.
  • Why do you want this role and our company?
  • Describe a challenge you faced and how you solved it.
  • Tell me about a time you worked in a team.
  • What are your strengths and weaknesses?
  • Where do you see yourself in 3 years?
  • Why should we hire you?
  • Tell me about a failure and what you learned.
  • How do you handle tight deadlines or pressure?

Technical Preparation Checklist

  • Revise data structures (arrays, strings, lists, maps).
  • Practice basic coding problems (loops, conditions, functions).
  • Review your projects: architecture, tech stack, challenges.
  • Understand basic OS, DB, and networking concepts.
  • Prepare 2–3 stories of debugging or problem solving.
  • Be ready to explain “why this tech stack?” for your projects.
  • Practice explaining complex technical topics in simple language.

Do's & Don'ts

  • Do: Maintain good eye contact and positive body language.
  • Do: Ask clarification if a question is unclear.
  • Do: Show your thought process while solving problems.
  • Do: Be honest about what you know and don't know.
  • Don't: Bluff about tools/skills you have never used.
  • Don't: Speak negatively about previous teachers/companies.
  • Don't: Argue with the interviewer; discuss politely instead.
  • Don't: Give one-word answers; add brief context.

Final Checklist Before the Interview

  • Resume printed / PDF updated and named professionally.
  • Projects revised & GitHub repos public and readable.
  • Company website and JD read carefully; note 3–4 key points.
  • Internet connection and devices checked (for online interviews).
  • Quiet environment arranged with minimal distractions.
  • 3–4 good questions ready to ask the interviewer.
  • Practice self-introduction 2–3 times out loud.
